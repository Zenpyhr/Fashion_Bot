Title: Track Is Back! Our Favorite Celebrities Are Embracing a Sporty Mood for Spring

Although there’s been a shift towards fashion maximalism this spring, with vibrant retro prints and high-impact color being top trends, we’ve noticed that many celebrities are embracing a vintage sport mood this season. There was no shortage of sport-coded looks on the spring/summer 2026 runways, with brands such as Prada, Loewe, and Lii Studio styling sporty jackets alongside everything from statement skirts to hot pants. It was only a matter of time before the trend made its way to the street—and many of our favorite celebs are adopting a subdued approach to wearing sporty pieces in real life.

Kendall Jenner recently stepped out in Los Angeles wearing her version of a deconstructed tracksuit. Starting with an oversized heritage jacket (a major runway talking point), she added a white sports bra and cotton poplin pants underneath, finishing with black Adidas Superstar sneakers—a throwback style we’re keeping eyes on, due for a revival. Meanwhile, Hailey Bieber did casual her way, styling a cropped technical zip-up with royal blue track pants, adding a sleek black kick from her extensive collection of sneakers and her signature oval-frame opticals.

However, you don’t have to reserve sporty pieces for solely off-duty moments or trips to and from the gym. During Fashion Month, we saw supermodel Paloma Elsesser pair her balloon-sleeved bomber with a sequin midi skirt and strappy Mary-Jane ballet shoes, whereas Alexa Chung opted for silky, lace-trimmed tap shorts alongside her funnel-neck windbreaker, accessorizing with Miu Miu kitten heels and a top-handle bag. Both outfits illustrate how a sporty jacket can take on a more feminine, dressed-up approach.

And if you’re looking for further proof to start incorporating something athletic into your spring outfits, Jennifer Lawrence swapped her go-to designer flip-flops from The Row for Adidas slide sandals (a steal at only $30), while out and about in New York City. The actress expertly mixed them with minimalist wardrobe staples: a white T-shirt, flowy satin pants, and a silk dress that was left billowy and undone to mimic a car coat —an editor-approved spring styling tip.
