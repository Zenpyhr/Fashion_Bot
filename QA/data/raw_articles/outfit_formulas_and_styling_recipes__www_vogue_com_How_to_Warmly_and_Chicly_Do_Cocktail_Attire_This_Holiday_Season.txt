Title: How to Warmly (and Chicly) Do Cocktail Attire This Holiday Season

It was an unexpectedly frigid night when Vogue.com Assistant Market Editor Anny Choi and I were meeting up to go out. On the schedule? A party with a dress code that called for cocktail attire. I’ve had my issues with dressing up in the past, but a cold snap provided an entirely different challenge: Bare one’s limbs to the elements in your favorite party dress or cover up and look like you didn’t make an effort? “One of the hardest things to do during the holidays is to dress warmly when you’re headed to cocktails,” Choi agreed. “In the winter, you always feel like you either look chic but suffer from the freezing cold or be warm—but look lazy.”

This is no way to live. But some people manage to pull it off, whether it’s a dash of fur or a fail-safe turtleneck, and we were sick of being left out in the cold. To find out how others are stylishly bracing for the frost from fete to fete, we tapped the Vogue.com crew for tips: Here, see what Vogue.com editors have to say about their cocktail dressing tips for fall, winter, and beyond, from unexpectedly chic-heeled Timberland boots to the other version of the LBD, the “little black suit.”
