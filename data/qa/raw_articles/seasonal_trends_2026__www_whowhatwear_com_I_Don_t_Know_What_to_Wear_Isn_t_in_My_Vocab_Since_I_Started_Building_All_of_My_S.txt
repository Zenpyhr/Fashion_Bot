Title: "I Don't Know What to Wear" Isn't in My Vocab Since I Started Building All of My Summer Outfits Around These 8 Elegant Pieces

As a fashion editor, I'm often asked for exciting, new shopping recommendations. And, while I'm so well versed in the latest pieces that finding hidden gems could be considered one of my love languages, I'm also an advocate for making the pieces you already own work harder. Building a balanced capsule wardrobe is not just frugal, it also helps to take the stress out of getting ready in the morning. Having an easy roster of pieces that you can mix and match at a moments notice is the secret to the most effortless ensembles, and summer is the perfect time to apply this approach.

Whether you're looking for practicality to face a sticky summer in the city, or packing light for a trip abroad, warm weather dressing is all about about ease. The less layers and fuss, the better, and after years of experience in dressing myself (and styling others), I always come back to the same pieces that champion comfort but without compromising on style.

This summer, like every other, I'm still investing in fresh basics, light linen and a couple of accessories that transition effortlessly between boardroom and beach, and I'm confident that you can condense everything you need to wear for the next few months into 8 simple categories. Intrigued as to how I do it? Keep scrolling to see the only items you need to build your summer capsule wardrobe in 2026.

Style Notes: Puff sleeves and full skirts are beautiful, and I've never seen a sweeping maxi I didn't like, but the best summer dresses are the ones that you can simply slip on and step out. Leave all of the bells and whistles to your choice of jewellery and accessories, and opt for dresses that are light, don't cling (and require minimal ironing). These are by far the easiest styles to wear with flat sandals and a basket bag by day, and a strappy heel and a mini bag by night, making them one of the most versatile pieces in your capsule wardrobe.

Whether you're wearing loose-legged trousers, airy skirts, pretty dresses or heatwave-proof shorts, there's nothing a pared-back sandal won't work with, and while rubber flip flops will always have a spot in my suitcase for poolside, investing in good quality leather and suede sandals ensure you'll have a pair you can rely on for years to come.

You may buy and wear them most during summer, but this year round heroes are also going to get a lot of play underneath your knitwear during autumn and winter too.

Style Notes: I love a roomy suede tote for everyday wear, but in summer basket bags feel like a natural evolution from winter's cosier fabrics. Just as spacious but with a little more artisanal energy, a quality basket bag is still trust worthy enough to carry all of your essentials (read, laptop, phone and keys), but equally works to hold your towels, books and SPF poolside too, how is that for duality? The enduring appeal of styles like Dragon Diffusion's triple jump and Loewe 's classic basket go to show how our appetite for woven bags hasn't waned over the years, so any bag you invest in now will be one tbat you pull out next year, and the next, and the next...

Style Notes: There's no better feeling than rolling out of bed, stepping out of the shower and straight into a pair of light linen trousers, and often, a co-ordinated shirt. We might all have the same pair of blue jeans that are our fallback failsafe, but in hot weather linen comes into it's own with a high comfort rating that can't be beaten. This summer you won't find me sweating in skinnies or peeling off leggings, linen always manages to have a breezy confidence that can be dressed up with a crisp shirt for work, or kept casual as a co-ord for your weekend errands. and, if a tailored wide-leg already has prime position in your wardrobe, a pair of baggy linen trousers will be an easy transition.

Style Notes: If I had to guess at just one piece that I know every fashion editor, influencer and industry insider has in their summer rotation, I'd confidently say an oversized shirt. Big shirts with rolled up sleeves and slouchy fits are all about the "borrowed from the boys" energy that implies that the wearer hasn't thought too much about their outfit, but still manages to look polished at a moment's notice. Toughened up with leather in autumn, worn under blazers and jumpers in winter, tucked into jeans in spring, and worn open over swimwear or dresses in summer, just like cotton basics these are clever investment (if not just for cost per wear alone), and their timelessness means they will never go out of style, but will only appreciate in sartorial value.

Oval, cat-eye and rectangular shades manage to add character to your outfit without taking away from the rest of the look, and after scaling down to my collection to just two pairs: one brown, and one black, I can confirm; the right pair really do go with everything.
