Title: The Lily Collins Guide to Tourist Style

Take a walk outside the Vogue offices and you’re liable to see dozens upon dozens of tourists milling about, dressed in a uniform of a white T-shirt, jean shorts, and running shoes. It’s hard to look put-together in a new city, especially when comfort is key.

Lily Collins and her husband, Charlie McDowell, however, proved that tourist dressing doesn’t need to make you stick out like a sore thumb. While out and about in London, the duo opted for somewhat matching outfits that helped them blend into their surroundings like seasoned proffesionals.

Collins and McDowell each opted for a dark gray sweater, which Collins wore over a light blue button-up shirt. They both wore jeans—his dark wash and hers light. The duo also donned khaki jackets: McDowell in a chore silhouette and Collins in a calf-length trench coat. She also carried a denim Diorstar hobo bag with a gold chain strap.

Ready for rain, the Emily In Paris star toted a small black umbrella while her husband covered his head with a baseball cap. And both chose comfortable, durable shoes that didn’t sacrifice their clout: she wore white sneakers, and he opted for Salomons.

Lily Collins and Charlie McDowell proved that it’s possible to be well prepared without looking like total dorks.
